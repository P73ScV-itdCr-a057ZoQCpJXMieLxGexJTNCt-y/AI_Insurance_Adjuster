02 / 12 The Problem

Claims are slow, inconsistent, and costly.

The traditional auto-claim process takes 7–14 days from submission to settlement. An adjuster physically inspects the vehicle, manually photographs damage, looks up repair costs, and writes estimates — all by hand.

Result: claimant dissatisfaction, high operating costs, and systematic fraud that human reviewers miss 60% of the time.

"The average auto claim costs $4,300 to process — half of that is labor, not repair costs."

04 / 12 Damage Detection

Computer vision trained on 180K claims.

YOLOv8-L fine-tuned on a proprietary dataset of annotated vehicle damage images. The model outputs bounding boxes, class labels, severity grades, and affected body panels — all in a single inference pass.

14 damage classes

Dent Scratch Crack Shatter Deformation Paint transfer Missing part Rust Flood mark Fire damage Hail Undercarriage Structural Totaled

Pre-processing matters

Raw phone photos pushed directly to the model score mAP 0.75. After CLAHE normalization + tiling: mAP 0.87 — a 12-point improvement purely from better inputs.

05 / 12 Cost Estimation

06 / 12 Fraud Detection

08 / 12 Adjuster Assist

AI augments. Humans decide.

The system is designed around a human-in-the-loop philosophy. Every AI estimate is a recommendation — the adjuster holds final authority, and every override becomes a training label for the next model version.
